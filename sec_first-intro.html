Section 0.3 Warmup Problems

Activity 0.1.

Solve:

\begin{align*} 3x_1-2x_2\amp= 6\\ -x_1+x_2\amp= 1 \end{align*}

Activity 0.2.

Draw a graph of the solution set of the equation: \(3x_1-2x_2=6\text{.}\)

Hint.

If a solution has \(x_1=a\text{,}\) what is \(x_2\) or viceversa?

Activity 0.3.

Draw a graph of the solution set of the equation: \(-x_1+x_2=1\text{.}\)

Activity 0.4.

Graph the solution sets from the two previous activities together. How does your answer to Question 1 compare to your graph?

Activity 0.5.

Solve:

\begin{align*} 2x_1-2x_2\amp=6\\ -x_1+x_2\amp=1 \end{align*}

Activity 0.6.

Solve:

\begin{align*} 2x_1-2x_2\amp=-2\\ -x_1+x_2\amp=1 \end{align*}

Question 0.7.

Wait… what just happened? Explain the results of the previous two problems. What do the graphs of the corresponding solution sets look like in relation to the graphs of the equations?

Question 0.8.

What are the possible intersections of two lines? Clearly state your conjecture.

Throughout this course we will be doing many of the same things you did in the previous questions, but we will do them in a more general setting that will allow us to solve \textbf{many} new and old problems.
